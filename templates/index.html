Education

  • Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D) @ Pierre and Marie Curie University
    2010 - 2014
    We developed a 'frequentist' alternative to best performing language models in natural language processing (Kneser-Ney smoothing) using structured penalties (tree-structured regularization). The above non-parametric statistical model was used with quicker optimization method (proximal gradient methods) and efficient data structures (suffix trees and max-heaps) to scale to millions of parameters. The results also throw light on their similarity in behavior to Hierarchical Pitman-Yor Process in Bayesian learning which performs similar to Kneser-Ney smoothing by producing distributions with 'sufficiently heavy tails. The method is generic and applies to any sequence of discrete symbols with the advantage that it can capture 'longer' Markov dependencies without 'overfitting' that were otherwise not possible. We also studied various techniques for hierarchical organization for classification with large number of classes, a computationally hard problem with wide application.
  • Master's @ Grenoble INP - UGA
    2009 - 2010
    We studied calibration of muti-camera systems by reducing the problem to solving polynomials in multiple variables similar to the classical '5-point algorithm' for two-camera systems. We used Grobner Basis methods and Buchberger's algorithm from algebraic geometry to find roots of the polynomials.
